This is a sandbox survival game that merges elements from two concepts:

  Ordovician survival (Concept 2) – a prehistoric, semi-realistic world as the foundation.
  Mathematical crafting system (Concept 4) – a unique core gameplay mechanic where mathematical expressions define item synthesis.
 The game avoids overcomplicated realism from Concept 2, while keeping symbolic, geometric, and mathematical systems as central features.

World

 Base Layer:
  The Ordovician world serves as the foundation.
  Oceans filled with invertebrates and cephalopods typical of the era.
  Land covered only with moss-like plants.

 Geometry-based terrain:
  All terrain is represented by geometric polygons (3–n sided).
  Dynamic actions (digging, erosion, etc.) are simulated by moving points in space with simplified algorithms.
  Terrain is stored using a tree/adjacency list structure. Each node has an ID, coordinates, connected nodes, and material data.

Chunks:
  The world is divided into chunks. Each chunk stores the last time it was loaded. When reloaded, the game   estimates what happened in the meantime instead of simulating everything continuously.

Visual Style

  Astroneer-like art direction but exclusively geometric (no smooth curves).
  Simple shading and lighting on polygonal surfaces.
  Creatures, NPCs, and plants are all geometric entities, clearly distinguishable from terrain (like how Minecraft mobs differ from blocks).

Player & Items

  The player species is a 10 cm amphibious octopus-like creature, unchanged from Concept 2.
  Core survival systems: oxygen, hunger, body temperature.

Inventory:

  Items are unique (not stackable).
  Each item has volume, weight, and durability.
  Backpack load affects movement speed.
  No “dropped item blocks”: When terrain is mined, the removed piece remains an irregular object in the storage tree, not a floating collectible. Powder-like materials appear as clusters of random internal points.

Crafting System

 Two layers of crafting:
  Ordinary Crafting Table – for real-world, simple items (sticks, tools, basic structures).
  Mathematical Crafting Table (core feature) – advanced crafting requires building complex mathematical expressions.
  Each item has a Mathematical ID.
  Basic items have simple IDs (e.g., 5, 2/3).
  Advanced items require complex expressions (e.g., |cos(10)√(π+8.72)|).
  Players cannot “split” values (e.g., 1 → 0.2 + 0.8). They must use subtraction or other operations to achieve values.

 Symbols as resources:
  Mathematical operators and functions (like +, −, cos()) must be gathered like normal resources.

 Rules for functions:
  Expressions like cos(60°) (rational outputs) cannot be used for high-tier crafting.
  Expressions like cos(59.6°) (irrational outputs) can be used since they remain function-based.

 Variables (later-game feature):
  These work like wildcards in crafting.
  e.g.:
   x: integers in range −100 to 100.
   y: decimals between −1000 and 5000, convertible to fractions.
   z: fractions with numerator/denominator < 1000, simplified value < 1.

Irrational Crafting Table (alternative system):
  Items have feature slots (e.g., material, size, shape).
  Players insert values/expressions, and an algorithm determines rarity/quality.
  Shapes can be generated automatically or adjusted by players via simple manual modeling.

Dimensions

  Predefined three extra dimensions (like the moon in Concept 2, but expanded).
  Later, players can generate new dimensions using rare crafting items (similar to Minecraft joke portals, but balanced with resource/feature control).
  Dimensions differ by resources and gameplay styles, not survival difficulty.

NPCs & Structures

 NPCs:
  More advanced than Minecraft villagers.
  Require food, care, and management to remain functional.
  Designed to feel like living alien beings integrated into the world.
  Some humanoid-like NPCs may appear via special relics.

 Structures:
  Symbolic monuments in real life (e.g., Notch pillar, Twin Towers ruins, Lincoln Hall).
  Act as rare landmarks with narrative meaning.
  From these, players can obtain unique resources like the “Life Seed” for creating NPCs.

Enemies & Gameplay

  Monsters are geometric creatures, fitting the abstract style.
  Entities are solid and cannot overlap (no glitch-like clipping).
  Core design includes player-friendly features:
  Navigation aids.
  Meme-style advancements/achievements.
  Symbolic main questline, similar in role to Minecraft’s.

Backend & Performance

  LaTeX storage format for mathematical expressions and crafting formulas.
  Heavy optimization needed to avoid performance collapse when handling complex expressions.
  Uses approximations for geometric volume calculations when exact math is too slow.

Overall

 This game blends:
  Ordovician survival sandbox (biology, environment, symbolic landmarks);
  Mathematical crafting system (expressions, irrational values, variable wildcards);
  Geometric aesthetic (terrain, creatures, objects built from polygons).
  The result is a sandbox survival-math hybrid where realism exists but does not dominate, and creativity comes from combining math with exploration and construction.
